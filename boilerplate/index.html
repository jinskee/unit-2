Historic Annual Snowfall

This map looks at 15 different ski areas in North America, and examines the annual snowfall from 2013-2021 at each ski area.

Use the sequence bar to check snowfall for different years and click on the symbols to retrieve the annual snowfall and to see a picture of the ski area.

Explore the map, find out which of these 15 ski areas has received the highest and lowest snowfall and which ski areas consistently receive the most snow. Once you've had some time to thouroughly investigate the map, pick your destination and plan a trip to one of these ski areas today!

The Data

The historic annual snowfall data comes from on the snow.

The images of the ski resorts on the pop-ups come from the ski areas websites:

Mad River Glen, Bridger Bowl, Mt. Baker, Mt. Bachelor, Whistler, Whitefish, Lost Trail, Brighton, Schweitzer, Taos Ski Valley, Brundage Mountain, Mt. Bohemia, Grand Targhee, Arapahoe Basin, Mammoth
